ⓘ How It Works

Tip Speed: Vtip = π × D × RPM / 60 - where D is propeller diameter in meters.

Mach Number: Vtip / speed of sound. Speed of sound = 331.3 + 0.606 × T(°C) m/s.

Theoretical RPM: KV × Voltage (no-load, no propeller attached).

Estimated Real RPM: Under load, actual RPM drops to roughly 60-80% of theoretical due to propeller drag, air resistance, motor efficiency losses, voltage sag, and prop weight/inertia. This tool uses 75% as a middle estimate.

Mach Zones: Below 0.62 - efficient and quiet. 0.62 to 0.88 - moderate, good balance. 0.88 to 0.92 - maximum thrust, tips near transonic. Above 0.92 - inefficient, shock waves at tips, airflow detaches, high noise.

Prop Tip Speed Help


Calculate the absolute physical speed at which your propeller tips cut through the air, and monitor transonic aerodynamic limits.


Understanding Transonic Limits:

  • As the tips of a propeller approach the speed of sound (Mach 1.0), the airflow detaches, forming micro shock waves. This leads to heavy turbulence, a massive drop in efficiency, and extremely loud prop wash.
  • Mach Zones:
    • Below 0.62: Super efficient and highly quiet.
    • 0.62 to 0.88: Moderate, excellent balance of thrust and sound.
    • 0.88 to 0.92: Max thrust zone. Transonic limits are reached.
    • Above 0.92: Severely inefficient. Heavy power is wasted as acoustic heat.
  • Air temperature alters the speed of sound. Adjust the environment slider to see how hot/cold climates alter your prop's aerodynamic limits.
