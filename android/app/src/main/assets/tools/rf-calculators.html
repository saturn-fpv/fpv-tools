RF Power Scale Rules of Thumb:
• +3 dB = Doubles milliwatt power output (e.g. 100mW ➔ 200mW is +3dB).
• +10 dB = 10x milliwatt power output (e.g. 100mW ➔ 1000mW is +10dB).
• -3 dB = Halves milliwatt power output.
• -10 dB = 1/10th milliwatt power output.

🔄 dB Change Parameters

dB
Examples of dB changes in FPV:
• +6 dB: Doubling transmitter power (e.g., 400mW to 800mW) OR upgrading standard 2dBi receiver omni to an 8dBi patch.
• +12 dB: Upgrading antenna gains by 12dBi total (e.g. standard omni 2dBi to high gain helical 14dBi).
• -6 dB: Flying behind trees/obstructions, or pointing polarization 90 degrees out of sync.

RF Calculators

Advanced diagnostic formulas for FPV radio & video transmission links

📡 Link Budget & Range
Uses the Free Space Path Loss (FSPL) equation to calculate the theoretical line-of-sight range. Received Power decreases by 6dB every time distance is doubled.
Note: Real-world conditions (humidity, ground reflections, Fresnel zone blockage, trees) typically degrade range by 30-50%.
⚡ mW ⟷ dBm Conversions
Converts linear RF power in milliwatts to logarithmic power in decibels (dBm). Logarithmic math makes calculating path attenuation simple: you just add antenna gains and subtract path loss.
⭕ Times Further (dB Delta)
Quickly calculates how range expands when modifying power or antennas. Every +6dB gain doubles your range in clean air, and increases the flyable coverage area by 4x (since area scales with the square of range).
📐 Antenna Design
Computes quarter-wave and half-wave element sizes for FPV radio equipment. Accounting for velocity factor (which is the speed of electric current propagation through conductors compared to light speed in a vacuum), it ensures maximum transmission efficiency and lowest VSWR.
