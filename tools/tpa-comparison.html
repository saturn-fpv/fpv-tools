About TPA (Throttle PID Attenuation): TPA reduces PID gains at high throttle to prevent oscillations. The breakpoint sets where attenuation begins, and the rate controls how much the PIDs are reduced above that point.

TPA Comparison Help


TPA (Throttle PID Attenuation) reduces PID gains at high throttle ranges to prevent mid-to-high throttle oscillations (often heard as high-frequency warbling or flutter at full punch-outs).


Understanding the inputs:

  • TPA Rate: The percentage by which the P and D gains are reduced at 100% throttle. E.g., a rate of 0.65 reduces your gains by 65% (PIDs retain 35% strength).
  • Breakpoint: The throttle value (in microseconds, from 1000 to 2000) where the attenuation starts. Below this breakpoint, PIDs retain 100% strength.
  • Use the dual control panels to overlay two different TPA profiles and compare how rapidly and aggressively they attenuate your gains.
