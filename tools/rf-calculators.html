Power Conversions Rules of Thumb:
• +3 dB = Doubles milliwatt power output (e.g. 100mW ➔ 200mW is +3dB).
• +10 dB = 10x milliwatt power output (e.g. 100mW ➔ 1000mW is +10dB).
• -3 dB = Halves milliwatt power output.
• -10 dB = 1/10th milliwatt power output.

RF Calculators

Diagnostic RF tools for FPV link budget range, power, antennas, Fresnel zones, cable loss, and VSWR.

Link Budget & Range
Uses the Free Space Path Loss (FSPL) formula to estimate range based on transmitter power, antenna gains, and receiver sensitivity.
Power Converter
Converts linear power in milliwatts to logarithmic decibels (dBm). Logarithmic math simplifies calculating system gain and path losses.
Times Further
Calculates range and flyable area coverage changes when altering antenna gains or transmitter power. Adding +6dB doubles range and quadruples flyable area.
Antenna Designer
Computes quarter-wave and half-wave elements size based on center frequencies and conductor velocity propagation speed.
Fresnel Zone Clearance
Calculates the clearance radius needed around the line-of-sight path. Obstructions in the first 60% of the Fresnel zone degrade link reliability.
Cable Loss
Calculates the signal attenuation over coaxial cable runs, scaled by frequency. Helps quantify losses in long pigtails or ground station lines.
VSWR Match
Measures antenna impedance matching. High VSWR reflects power back to the VTx output stages as heat, lowering output and risking damage.
